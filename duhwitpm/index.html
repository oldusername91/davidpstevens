Australia's Top Job

The Prime Minister (The PM) is the leader of government in Australia. Being the leader of the government means many things:

  • The PM generally has the last say in making decisions for the government through Cabinet
  • The PM is the head of the National Security Committee
  • The PM is the leader of the legislative branch of government

Benefits and Salary

The Prime Minister gets a generous salary and many other benefits that make the top job a rewarding position. It can be similar to being the CEO of a company.

  • Salary - $500,000 a year
  • While in Office, other generous entitlements free housing and utilities, free international travel and free meals
  • Pension – Around $300,000 for the rest of their life

Career after Politics

Once a PM’s term has ended either by election or being replaced through the party system, the PM can choose to follow a number of career paths. Some of the most common are listed below:

  • Consultant or Board Member for organisations or businesses
  • Continue as a Member of Parliment (if possible)
  • Ambassador or Foreign Consulate for Australia abroad

Did you know?

Higher education is not essential to becoming Prime Minister, two notable examples being:
  • John Curtin
  • Paul Keating

After School

Almost all PM’s have finished school and then proceeded to get a higher education at a university. Some study overseas while others study within Australia. The most common degree that PMs have chosen to study is law.

The universities with the most graduates who went on to being the Prime Minister are:

  • Sydney University
  • Melbourne University
  • Oxford University

Did you know?

Two Prime Minister's that achieved high places in student associations:
  • John Howard
  • President of the Young Liberals
  • Julia Gillard
  • Vice President of the Australian Union of Students

Getting Involved

Most PMs while at university have been actively involved in student unions and organisations, such as newspapers. Through this they have learned to network and deal with a range of personalities. This has helped them further on in their chosen careers. In addition to this, most former PMs have been involved in the youth arm of their chosen political party.

Joining a political party

Youth Parties

  1. Young Labor
  2. Australian Labor Party
  1. Young Liberals
  2. Liberal Party of Australia
  1. Young Greens
  2. Australian Greens

This is the first real step into politics that an ambitious young person will need to take on their path to becoming prime minister. First, one must be a member of the party of their choice. Generally this involves a joining fee and one must attend meetings of the local branch. The branch votes and nominates one of their local branch members to run as the party candidate for the electorate at the Federal election. However all major parties have their own youth movements specifically for young members.

Be the Party Leader

ALP Leadership Ballot

Requirements for an ALP leadership change

  1. 51% of rank-and-file (non-MPs) vote
  2. 51% of the caucus (MPs).

Probably the hardest step in the path to becoming PM is winning the national leadership of your political party. It involves many deals and promises with party faction leaders in order to get the support required. In most cases though, the opportunity for gaining the leadership occurs after a political party loses an election and they are looking for a new leader who ultimately could become PM. But occasionally the party sees a need for a leadership change while they are in government.

Getting Elected

Election Campaign

  1. Tour the country
  2. Make promises to voters
  3. Run advertisements
  4. Debate the opposing party leaders

If you gain the party leadership while in opposition, then your party must win the most seats in Parliament at the next Federal Election. Before an election, you will run a campaign in order to convince voters that you are the best choice for the leader of the nation. On Election Day, the polls will open in the morning and usually before the day has ended, you will know if you have become Australia’s next Prime Minster.
